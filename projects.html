Previous Adventures in Making

The moaning dinosaur™

Walking with a friend, we spotted an electric dinosaur on a chair. I grabbed it, checked its roar function (it worked!), but craved more.

A plan hatched! I had just gotten a funny audio file - a moan - from another friend. "Moaning dinosaur!" I thought. Determined, I ordered a cheap Chinese board that played uploaded audio with a button press. It worked perfectly upon arrival!

Disassembling the dinosaur revealed a seemingly simple setup: a jaw button, speaker, and battery compartment. Wiring it all together went smoothly, but fitting it back in the case proved tricky.

Using Solidworks, I 3D modeled a custom case to fit between the freely-rotating body and tail. After 3D printing, I crammed everything in, screwed it together, and voila - a moaning dinosaur! The battery drains fast, but hey, a moaning dinosaur is worth it, right?

My First Solo Lathing Endeavor: A Wooden Ball

April 24 2024. This was the first time I used a lathe. I wanted to make a wooden ball, so I asked my woodworking teacher how. He told me that we needed to glue some pieces of wood together. I agreed. I got to work and within 10 minutes I had a big block of wood that needed to dry. The class ended and I went home. Two days later, on the 26 of april 2024. I was back in the shop with my dried wood. The edges of the wooden block were a bit unstraight, so he straightened them in his table saw. From there, he showed me how to do it and then let me take over. I stood there for about an hour carving it to perfection. I got the surface as good as I could. Even though it was less of a ball and more of an egg, I was happy with it. I put some oil on it, took it out of the machine, and went home with a wooden ball (I also forgot my watch at school, which was a bit annoying).

    Raspberry Pi Nature Camera

    Raspberry Pi Nature Camera: This project popped into my head after I installed a birdhouse in the garden. I really wanted to be able to look inside whenever I wanted without disturbing the birds. I'd get a Raspberry Pi Zero W (because of the small size and internet connectivity) connect a Raspberry Pi Camera to it, and then either get it to stream an RTSP (Real-time Streaming Protocol) stream viewable through software like Agent DVR or OBS. But I could also install MotionEyeOS because it's simpler. I guess I'll figure that out in the future. (note: the bird house is now getting molested by cats)
